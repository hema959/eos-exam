Dindigul Biryani

Originating from a small town called Dindigul in Tamil Nadu, the Dindigul biryani offers a unique and highly enriching biryani-tasting experience. It is the locals' favourite and whether you like biryani or not, you ought to try it. You can order it from your favourite biryani destination, Ekdum, and enjoy the South Indian delicacy. The Dindigul biryani stands out from its competitors as it satisfies all your five senses with every morsel. Whether you are trying it for the first time or the 100th, you will surely be left feeling happy and blessed at the end of the meal.

Ambur Biryani

Ambur biryani is a popular dish from the town of Ambur in Tamil Nadu, India. It's known for its unique taste, which comes from a special blend of spices, herbs, and other ingredientsThe dish is said to have been introduced by the Arcot Nawabs, and was refined by Hasin Baig, a cook in their royal kitchens.

Hyderabadi Biryani

Hyderabadi biryani is a famous dish from Hyderabad, India that's known for its unique cooking process, aroma, and signature taste: Hyderabadi biryani originated in the kitchens of the Nizam of Hyderabad and is a blend of Hyderabadi and Mughlai cuisines. It's believed to have come to India from Persia and has been a part of Hyderabadi cuisine for over 400 years.

Kerala Biryani

This dish, which was served to the soldiers of the Chera kings in Kerala, was said to be made of rice, ghee, meat, turmeric, coriander, pepper, and bay leaf. The spices used in the preparation of South Indian biryanis, such as those used in the Malabar variety, are distinctive to the region and the Western Ghats.

Bombay Biryani

A regional take on the classic, Bombay Biryani has inherited its name from present-day Mumbai. Striking a harmonious balance between sweet, sour and spicy, Bombay Biryani gets its distinctive flavour from the use of dried plums and fried potatoes Indian cuisine is a colorful tapestry of flavors, and one dish that stands out with its captivating blend of spices and aromatic rice is the legendary Bombay Biryani. Originating in the bustling streets of Mumbai, this culinary masterpiece has won hearts and palates worldwide.

Bangalur Biryani

Seeraga samba rice is a short-grain, aromatic rice variety that is popular in South India. It is known for its distinct fragrance and is often used to prepare biryanis and pulao. Seeraga samba is the preferred rice for making this biryani.
